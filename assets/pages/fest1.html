Chelsea Flower Show

The RHS Chelsea Flower Show is a popular flower festival that is held every year in late May. Held at the Royal Chelsea Hospital grounds, the festival lasts for 5 days with a variety of activity and events happening during the daytime. There is a sell-off during the last day in which attendees have the chance to purchase their dream flower or flowers that suit their back garden.

Members who are part of the RHS have two days of early access to visit the show. This allow them to view the displays before the public and in an uncrowded environment. In fact, it is useful for those who wish to see the gardens but have a packed schedule. There are a variety of flowers available with some of them used to create beautiful displays. These displays are sometimes sold to the public on the final day for the public to use them personally.

Given that this is the first time you have heard of them, you might think that it has been around for a number of years. Actually, it has been around for decades. We will show you why many generations have visited the show.



The show was held at the hospital grounds since 1913, but its origins is much deeper. It was first held at Chiswick in 1833 but moved to Kensington in 1863 in a bid to improve visiting numbers and provide better transportation. It ran under the name "The Royal Horticultural Society Great Spring Show". After hosting there for 26 years, The Great Show moved to London where Temple Gardens was chosen as the next location. Eventually, it became a sucess after requisitioning three marquees from the former site. But one critical show shaped the future of the festival as a whole.

In 1912, the Royal International Horticultural Exhinition took place at the Royal Hospital in Chelsea. The site proved to be suitable for shows like the aforementioned and the Great Spring Show moved there from 1913 onwards. As time passed by, the show gradually increased in popularity. In fact, it became so popular that overcrowding issues arose. Despite the issues, the show continues to become a place for many independent and commercial firms to showcase their designs.



Throughout the years, the Chelsea Flower Show has seen many familiar visitors, including those from the Royal Family, such as Queen Elizabeth II, King Charles III, and Catherine, Princess of Wales. The image and formality of the shows meant that it has been synonymous among the rich. After all, the displays shown is treated as a form of art. To provide motivation to the firms and continue increase the number of visitors, awards were introduced wich each category being nominated for at least one award. There are also special awards given to unique and creative designs, displays, or art.

As well as that, there have been significant exhibit to help attract modern visitors and stary relevant. Today, the show sees 157,000 visitors every year and continues to expand its awards and gardens. It is a festival that is enjoyable, if you like flowers and art, and that continues to become inovative.
