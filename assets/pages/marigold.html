Marigold

The marigold is a flower known for its vibrant gold and orange tones, bringing warmth and energy to gardens and rituals. It’s often associated with the sun due to its bright appearance and symbolizes creativity and *positivity*. Marigolds have played important roles in cultural traditions across the world, from India to Latin America. In Day of the Dead celebrations, their scent and color guide spirits home. They are also used in *weddings*, garlands, and sacred offerings. With their ease of growth and strong aroma, marigolds are both decorative and practical in the garden.

Beyond beauty, marigolds are valued for their medicinal and pest-repelling properties. They can be made into infusions, oils, and balms. The petals are sometimes edible and used to color and flavor food. *Calendula*, a close relative, is known for soothing skin. These flowers thrive in full sun and poor soil, requiring minimal care. Their presence deters insects and even nematodes underground. Marigolds bloom generously and respond well to regular trimming. They’re a symbol of resilience and cheerfulness, reminding us that strength and beauty often grow from humble soil.
